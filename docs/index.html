About

Our goal is to send eight men and six women to represent the Canadian delegation at the 2022 World Skate Games in Argentina.
  • When? October 24, 2022 - November 13, 2022
  • Where? San Juan, Argentina

    • Check out the 2022 World Skate Games Official Event Page for more info

    • What is Canada Skateboard?

      Canada Skateboard is the national body for skateboarding in Canada, recognized by the Canadian Olympic Committee and World Skate. The organization is responsible for promoting skateboarding supporting long-term skateboarder development, organizing a national event series, and managing participation at international events.

    • What is the World Skate Games?

      Overseen by World Skate, the World Skate Games consists of 12 sports, and ~7000 athletes representing 80 countries, the highest level of competition in the world. \ This will be the third World Skate-sanctioned Championships of Vert, the second one for Downhill and Street Luge. And, it will be the first ever World Skate-sanctioned World Championships for Slalom Skateboarding.

    • Canada holds the defending Championship

      In 2019, Canadian Dane Hanna brought home the Gold, taking 1st place in Barcelona, Spain. Post-pandemic, the WSG is back and the Canadian Downhill Team plans to earn that Gold once again.

    • Our Downhill Team Needs Your Help

      Unfortunately, without Government funding, a journey to the World Skate Games is a cost not all of the athletes on our team can afford. We ask for charitable donations to help subsidize the cost of our safety equipment, travel expenses, and accommodation.

    • Sponsorships & Charitable Donations

      We understand that you have several choices when considering different marketing and community outreach opportunities. By assisting our athletic pursuits, the Canadian Downhill Team will have the opportunity to defend our title, as we compete against the Worlds fastest and finest Downhill Skateboarders.

    • Will my donation qualify for the charitable tax credit?

      Yes! Canada Skateboard is a not-for-profit corporation (703050294RR0001), and a Registered Canadian Amateur Athletic Association . We use Charitable Impact to accept donations and generate a donation receipt for your tax return.

    Dane Hanna

    • Name: 'Danger' Dane Hanna
    • Age: 24
    • Hometown: Pender Harbour, BC
    • Stance: Regular
    • Sponsors: Landyachtz, Dont Trip Skateboards, Venom Skate, Cariboo Brewing, Rogers Bros DH, Team Irene

    Born and raised in Pender Harbour, British Columbia, Dane Hanna made his international debut in 2019 and soon became a household name in the world of downhill skateboarding. Holder of the World Champion title, Hanna won the previous WRG in 2019, and was crowned the International Downhill Federations North American Champion the same year. Having already won one world championship, Dane is eager to defend his title at the 2022 World Roller Games, and once again bring home the gold for Canada.

    Growing up in Pender Harbour, home to Danger Bay -- the world's first and longest-running downhill skateboarding race, Dane began his athletic career with Team Irene at the age of 10. Certainly, this contributed to Dane becoming one of the world's fastest downhill skateboarders, and a three-time Danger Bay champion. That may be why he's called Danger Dane, or it may be something else.

    Tessa Campbell

    • Name: Tessa Campbell
    • Age: 26
    • Hometown: West Vancouver, BC
    • Stance: Goofy
    • Sponsors: Landyachtz, Bear Trucks, Hawgs Wheels, Supra Distribution, Tality Wellness, Team Irene

    Tessa is pretty much the human version of a golden retriever. Always a little too hyped and happy to be here!

    She grew up in Vancouver on the North Shore, spending her summers in Pender Harbour. Although both of those locations have a booming downhill scene, skateboarding was not her first love. Growing up she did a wide plethora of sports, many of them on boards, just not on the pavement... Which is why there is no surprise when she stepped on a skateboard and was completely obsessed. It was in Pender Harbour where downhill skateboard group, Team Irene, showed her how to bomb hills. The speed of downhill skateboarding and the exhilaration of racing got her hooked. She is very excited to compete in the upcoming World Skate Games as a Canadian athlete. Her goal, aside from winning, is to inspire non-traditional skateboarders and bring as many people into the sport as she can.

    Amie Sheppard

    • Name: 'Skate Shepp' Amie Sheppard
    • Age: 32
    • Hometown: Caledon, ON
    • Stance: Regular
    • Sponsors: Mom

    In 2013 I produced the documentary film "Spread the Stoke" for my Thesis Project at McMaster University, Hamilton ON. I won the University Thesis Award for Multimedia with this film about the international female longboard community and found skateboarding after a lifetime of varsity athletics. I won female athlete of the year grade 7, 8 and 9 in Caledon, ON. I competed with McMaster University Varsity Rowing team in 2008/09 and the McMaster Varsity Rugby team in 2009/10 and 2010/2011.

    I began Downhill Skateboard racing in 2014 when I moved to BC. I placed 1st in the 2014 Skateboard Biathalon (Skate & Shoot) in Whistler, BC. I enjoyed lots of racing through 2014 to 2016 and am excited to be back in the scene to represent Canada at the 2022 World Skate Games. Downhill Skateboarding has been an incredibly positive influence on my life. It has helped me overcome mental struggles and build lifelong friendships.
